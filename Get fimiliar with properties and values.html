Urban legends are stories that are often mysterious, frightening, or humorous, and are sometimes used to teach a lesson. They can be told as complete stories with characters and plots.

Examples of urban legends:

The Slender Man

A tall, faceless figure dressed in black with tentacle-like appendages, Slender Man is said to stalk children, often leading them away from their homes. Although it originated as an internet meme, it has since become a widely known urban legend with tragic real-life events connected to it.

The Bloody Mary Ritual

This legend claims that if you stand in front of a mirror in a dark room and chant "Bloody Mary" three times, a ghostly figure will appear, sometimes to harm you. The story has been used as a spooky game for children daring each other to face the myth.

The Haunted Phone Number

In this legend, someone calls a random phone number and hears eerie voices or strange occurrences on the other end. Some versions suggest that the number connects to the spirits of the dead, while others imply it is cursed or tied to an unsolved mystery.
